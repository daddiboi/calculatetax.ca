Pass Your Written Permit Test in California

In California, securing a learner's permit is an exciting journey to transform into an accountable driver. The process of getting a learner's permit is a bit lengthy, but if you do things step by step, it will get easy. In this, you start from learning the rules and regulations to give your knowledge test; after that, you drive under the supervision of someone who is experienced. Lastly, you make yourself capable of becoming an independent driver by achieving a full driver's licence. Let's see all these steps in more detail.

Obtaining a driver's permit became a part and parcel role in everyone's life. The very first step in getting a learner's permit is to apply for that in California. The required age for applying for a permit is 15.5 years, which denotes that if your age is under 15, you are not eligible for the permit. Furthermore, if your age is 18, then it is mandatory for you to get permission from your parents or guardians while applying for the learner's permit.

The second step requires getting knowledge of the rules and regulations of California, and based on that, you are required to give a knowledge test to get a learner's permit. You can prepare for the exam from PermitDMV, as it provides all the questions that appear on the exam. This includes all the traffic rules, safety tips, traffic signs and laws of driving. When you solve the practice tests, you will also get an idea about the format and questions that appear on the written test. A common saying is "Practice makes a man perfect”; if you practice more for an exam, then for sure there are high chances for you to be successful.

Once you are confident and have made up your mind for the written test, you are needed to collect all the mandatory documents required for applying for the permit test. These documents are listed below:

Required Documents:

  • Identity Proof (includes passport or birth certificate)
  • Residency Proof of California
  • Social Security Number

After this, you are required to fill out a form known as DL 44. You can get this form by visiting the DMV office or online on their website, which makes your process easier and quicker. Now, as everything is done, including your documents and preparation for the exam, you can either book an appointment at your nearby DMV office or directly go to the office. It's always better to book an appointment rather than waiting in long queues for walk-ins.

Let's talk about the written exam structure. It is a computer-based exam which includes only multiple-choice questions. There is a total of 46 MCQs, out of which you need to give a minimum of 38 correct answers to pass the exam. Along with that, the time limit to complete this exam is 60 minutes. In addition, before the written test you will also go through the eye test and verification of your ID's.

This is the time when you get curious about the exam scores. If you pass the exam, you will get your learner's permit, and you are allowed to practice driving under the guidance of a person who is 25 years old, along with that that person must hold experience of 50 hours of driving if your age is under 18 years.
